ü§î What is DNS?

DNS = Domain Name System

DNS records basically tell people‚Äôs computers how to get to your website, email, etc.

Brief History of DNS

  • Back in the day, there was a hosts file
  • Hosts file(s) became too much to maintain
  • DNS solves this problem
  • Kind of like putting the hosts file on the internet

Understanding your DNS Configuration

Key Terminology

Registrar

Company or organization that registers your domain. They point to your name servers.

Nameservers

Where your DNS records are hosted. DNS records point to your host.

Hosts & Service Providers

Your host is where your website lives.
(Or we could be talking about a service provider, like in the case of email or a digital marketing tool).

What DNS Records Look Like

A records

						
							
							NAME             TYPE  POINTS TO
							
							@          IN    A     172.134.55.67    ; example.com
							employees  IN    A     172.134.55.67    ; employees.example.com
							store      IN    A     164.55.78.125    ; store.example.com

Company.com and employees.company.com are on the same server. Store.company.com is hosted elsewhere.

MX records

							
								NAME           TYPE  TTL   POINTS TO
								@        IN    MX    1     alt1.aspmx.l.google.com.    ; Gsuite / Gmail
								@        IN    MX    1     aspmx.l.google.com.
								@        IN    MX    5     alt2.aspmx.l.google.com.    ; Another Gsuite server
								@        IN    MX    10    alt3.aspmx.l.google.com.
								@        IN    MX    10    alt4.aspmx.l.google.com.
								store    IN    MX    0     store.example.com.
								store    IN    A           164.55.78.125               ; Another email service

Email for Company.com goes to Gsuite. Gsuite has multiple different mail servers defined. Email for store.example.com gets routed to the same server that hosts that website.

CNAME records

							
								NAME           TYPE     POINTS TO
								www      IN    CNAME    example.com.    ; www.example.com - example.com
								demo     IN    CNAME    sbddesign.github.io.    ; demo.example.com - GitHub Page 
								mail     IN    CNAME    example.com    ; mail.example.com - example.com

www.example.com goes to example.com. demo.example.com points to a microsite hosted on GitHub pages. mail.example.com points back to example.com. Note that we are not altering the handling of the email in any way just by adding a subdomain called “mail”.

TXT records

							
								NAME                  TYPE     VALUE
								example.com.    IN    TXT      "google-site-verification=pZBjhabcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyz"    ; Google site verification
								example.com.    IN    TXT      "v=spf1 include:spf.protection.outlook.com include:salesforce.com ~all"    ; SPF Records

Text records store unstructured data related to your domain. Frequently, it's used for things like verification with sites and service-providers (e.g. Google) or using SPF to protect your emails (more on that later).

Problems & Solutions

Problem: Migrating a Website

You‚Äôre trying to migrate to a new host and are worried about experience downtime.

Solution: Stage to a Subodmain

Use your DNS to establish a subdomain. Setup site at new host under the subdomain and get it running. Once running, point the main domain to same place as subdomain. When the propagation is complete, users will seamlessly be directed to the cloned site without noticing. üòé

Problem: Lost Records

Your host cancels your account. Or your host messes something up. Or a malicious threat cyber-actor [insert current jargon] hacks the crap out of your zone file. üò±

Problem: Security Breach

Solution: Basic Security Hygiene

Pay close attention to who has access to your DNS records. Don‚Äôt give out the password all willy nilly. 2FA. Basic security hygiene. üîê

Problem: Site Too Slow

Can be due to the host (most common, in my personal experience). But also people have to do a DNS lookup to get there, and slow DNS just adds to the time it takes. üò¥

Solution: Premium DNS

Switch to a professional DNS provider that‚Äôs separate from your registrar or host. üî•

Problem: Email going to spam

Your mail is not coming from the same server as your website is hosted on. So any email from your WP site goes to spam. üí©

Solution #1: SPF Records

This establishes at the DNS level which servers can send mail on your behalf (with the IP, not the domain). Add any server you want to send mail from to the SPF records. üìß

Solution #2: 3rd Party Mail Service

They will want you to modify your DNS records more than likely if you wan them to send mail from a domain you own. Will vary from provider to provider, but will likely involve adding MX records and TXT records. üì©

Further Trust: Add DKIM keys üîë

Your digital marketing tools, like SalesForce, MailChimp, etc. may send mail as your domain. Some of these will support DKIM as well. Basically, they can digitally sign anything they send using a private key. The public key (which is used to verify the signature) is published on your DNS. Thus, any computer the receives an email can verify the authenticity of it by verifying the digital signature which what‚Äôs published in the DNS.
